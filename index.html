📐 Wide-swing (Sooch) vs 2-stack cascode — the headroom trade
Both mirrors are two NMOS tall (a cascode), so both get the ~gmro2 output resistance. The 2-stack cascode self-biases its cascode gate to 2·Vgs, wasting ≈Vt on the bottom device and starving the output cascode (Vds = 0.15 V → Rout only 5.5 MΩ). The Sooch wide-swing mirror adds one ¼-size bias device that drops the cascode gate to Vt+2Vov, seating the bottom pair at Vdsat — recovering ~0.4 V of output swing and giving ~5× the Rout (26 MΩ) at mid-rail. Full ngspice write-up, annotated schematics and reproducible testbenches in the repo.
