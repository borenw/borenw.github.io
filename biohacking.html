Top 10 popular — but they're traps

Widely evangelized online, low actual yield (or actively counterproductive). Ordered by how much attention they steal relative to what they return.

TRAP 01

NMN / NAD⁺ boosters for longevity

Dramatic results are from mice. Human trials raise NAD⁺ markers but show no gain in strength, muscle, or lifespan.

Instead: creatine + sleep + lifting — proven, and cheaper.[9]

TRAP 02

Semen retention for "energy"

No physiological mechanism supports it. Regular ejaculation has some protective data. The "power" felt is placebo + discipline.

Instead: keep the discipline, drop the biology claim.

TRAP 03

Lemon-water "detox"

Your liver and kidneys already detox. Lemon water is just flavored water with trace vitamin C.

Instead: plain hydration + electrolytes on keto.

TRAP 04

Extreme OMAD (protein-starved)

One meal makes hitting 1.6–2.2 g/kg protein nearly impossible — so you lose muscle while thinking you're optimizing.

Instead: keep the fast, front-load protein.[2]

TRAP 05

Keto as a moral rule

A useful tool for some, dogma for none. Food quality and protein matter more than macro purity.

Instead: judge by bloodwork, not ideology.

TRAP 06

Ice bath right after lifting

Cold immediately post-strength blunts the hypertrophy signal — you pay for the plunge with lost gains.

Instead: separate cold from lifting by 4–6h.

TRAP 07

Red-light / infrared as cure-all

Narrow real effects (skin, some recovery) get sold as whole-body optimization. Mostly marketing gloss.

Instead: treat as a nice-to-have, not a pillar.

TRAP 08

15-pill supplement stacks

Cost and complexity balloon; evidence for most is thin. The stack becomes identity, not medicine.

Instead: creatine, omega-3, D/Mg if low. Stop.[7]

TRAP 09

"Cleanse" / juice programs

No toxin is removed that your organs don't already handle. Often just under-eating with a story attached.

Instead: fiber + whole food, permanently.

TRAP 10

Chasing biohacks over basics

Tape, plunges and gadgets feel productive while sleep, alcohol and protein — the real levers — go unmanaged.

Instead: fix #1–9 before buying anything.[1][4]
